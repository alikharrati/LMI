Overview of the Accommodation and Food Services Sector

New Brunswick Sector Snapshot: Accommodation and Food Services

Introduction

This section provides an overview of the current status and future projections of the accommodation and food services sector in New Brunswick (N.B.).

Types of Businesses

Businesses in this sector include:

  • Restaurants and limited-service eating places
  • Drinking establishments
  • Special food services (e.g., catering)
  • Accommodation services (e.g., hotels, RV parks)

Employment Chart

The number of people employed in the accommodation and food services sector from 2013 to 2023:

  • 2013: 24,500
  • 2019: 25,400
  • 2020: 21,100 (COVID-19 impact)
  • 2022: 21,400 (Slight recovery)
  • 2023: 21,200

Job Vacancy Rate 2023

The job vacancy rate in 2023 reflects high demand for labor in this sector:

  • New Brunswick: 6.4%
  • Canada: 6.8%

Employer Optimism Q2 2024

Employer optimism regarding the future of this sector:

  • Very Optimistic: 14%
  • Somewhat Optimistic: 46%
  • Somewhat Pessimistic: 18%
  • Very Pessimistic: 5%
  • Unknown: 17%

Common Occupations

Common occupations in this sector include:

  • Food counter attendants, kitchen helpers, and related support occupations
  • Cooks
  • Restaurant and food service managers
  • Food and beverage servers
  • Food service supervisors

Real GDP Growth

The real GDP in this sector declined at an annual growth rate of -3.6% from 2017 to 2022 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Long-Term Outlook (2023-2032)

Forecasted job opportunities in this sector between 2023 and 2032:

  • Retirements: 3,759
  • New jobs due to changes in demand: 2,099
  • Total job opportunities: 5,859

Union Coverage, Education Levels, and Average Hourly Wage

Key statistics:

  • Union Coverage: 4%
  • No Post-Secondary Education: 63%
  • Technical/Vocational Certificate: 25%
  • University Degree: 13%
  • Average Hourly Wage in New Brunswick: $16.50
  • National Average Hourly Wage: $19.12
